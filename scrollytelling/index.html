Women are Flourishing in the Work Industry
...but not in Tech
Currently, women make up 56% of the workforce
But despite the increase in working women 74% of all STEM professionals are men
But it wasn't always this way
In 1990, women in tech grew to a still unacceptable 35%, but it was progress
Since then women in tech have since dropped to an astounding 26%
Maybe it's time we start asking ourselves why
Is it fair that women consistently get paid 77% of what men get paid?
Who thinks companies should promote more gender diversity?
Of those who choose tech, how many leave tech mid-career?
Who is subject to sexual harrassment or other unwanted advances?
Right. Now let's take a look at some of our favorite tech giants
30% of all Google employees are women - but only 17% are hold tech related positions
Twitter's workforce is comprised of 33% of women but has an almost laughable 10% of women in tech related positions
Women's prescence in tech is dwindling
The number one issue holding women back is stereotypes.
Tech culture has fostered an environment in which women are percieved as inadequate or less than.
The question “What do you think your salary should be?” should be abolished, women consistently ask for less than men. Interviewers should provide a fair and transparent salary range.
Make gender equality part of training and education for both men and women
We need to be proactive about welcoming women
It's about time we address this problem, for the future women of tech
